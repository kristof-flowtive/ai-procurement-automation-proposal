An AI-driven workflow that takes every job from materials schedule to paid invoice, with you approving each buy in one click.

What's Likely Slowing You Down

Committing £700k a year without a dedicated buyer leaves gaps in pricing, process, and paperwork. Here is where that usually shows up.

Materials schedules sit in Buildertrend while someone on the team manually chases quotes by email

Supplier quotes arrive in different formats and comparing them line-by-line eats hours per job

No single view of what was ordered, what arrived, and what was invoiced against each job

Invoices get paid without being properly matched against the original purchase order

Xero entries lag behind real purchasing activity, making cashflow harder to read

Without a buyer, the business carries pricing risk, delivery risk, and invoice-matching risk at the same time

Proposed Solution

I will build a full AI procurement workflow in n8n that mirrors the process a good buyer would run. When a materials schedule is finalised in Buildertrend, the system drafts quote requests to your preferred suppliers, parses their replies, produces a ranked recommendation, and waits for your one-click approval before anything is ordered.

Once approved, the purchase order is generated and sent to the winning supplier, the order is logged against the job, deliveries are chased, and invoices are matched line-by-line against the PO. Approved POs and matched invoices flow straight into Xero. You stay in control of every financial decision while the admin runs itself.

How This Will Work

Seven steps from materials schedule to paid invoice, with one human decision in the middle.

1
Materials schedule is finalised in Buildertrend and triggers the workflow
2
Claude drafts tailored quote requests and emails your preferred suppliers
3
Supplier replies are parsed into a structured, line-by-line comparison
4
A ranked recommendation with reasoning is sent to you via email or WhatsApp
5
One click approves the buy, rejects it, or asks for another round of quotes
6
PO is auto-generated, sent to the winning supplier, and logged against the job
7
Deliveries are chased automatically and invoices matched to POs before Xero sync
